Care for your Fursat

Simple steps to keep your handwoven pieces beautiful for years.

Handwoven textiles are living fabrics — they breathe, soften, and become more beautiful with time. A little extra care goes a long way.

🌊

Hand Wash

Cold water, mild detergent. Gently swirl — never scrub or twist.

🌿

Dry in Shade

Press water out gently. Dry flat in shade — direct sun fades natural dyes.

♨️

Low Iron

Iron on low heat while slightly damp. Use a pressing cloth for delicate weaves.

🧴

Mild Detergent

Use ph-neutral, colour-safe detergent. Avoid bleach entirely.

🏪

Dry Clean

For silk and very delicate pieces, professional dry cleaning is safest.

📦

Store Folded

Store folded, not hung. Breathable cotton bags preferred over plastic.

Fabric-Specific Care

Khadi & Handloom Cotton — Machine wash on a gentle cycle is fine, but hand washing extends the life. These fabrics get softer with every wash.

Viscose & Viscose-Linen Blends — Always hand wash. Viscose loses strength when wet — never wring. Dry flat immediately.

Chanderi Silk — Dry clean only, or very gentle hand wash with no agitation. Do not soak for more than 5 minutes.

Natural Dyes — Wash separately for the first 2–3 washes as natural dyes may bleed slightly. This is normal and part of the natural dye process.

Pilling

Some natural-fibre garments may pill slightly after heavy wear. A fabric shaver removes pilling easily and restores the look of the garment. This is not a defect — it's a characteristic of handspun yarn.
